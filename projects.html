3. Increasing Efficiency of Rankine Cycle

The Rankine Cycle is a thermodynamic model consisting of four stages: compression, heat addition, expansion, and heat rejection. It illustrates the transformation of water into steam to generate power. The cycle begins with water compression, followed by isobaric heating in a boiler, expansion through a turbine, and concludes with isobaric heat rejection in a condenser. While idealized, the Rankine cycle provides fundamental insights into the efficiency of steam-based power systems, guiding the analysis and optimization of practical applications.

4. Transient Heat Analysis of Car Brake System

This project focuses on understanding the thermal behaviour of a car's brake system during braking events. The project aims to simulate and analyze how heat is distributed and dissipated within the brake components over time. It utilizes Numerical Methods (Finite Difference Method) and Computational Modelling to gain insights into the Transient Thermal Dynamics of the brake system. It also discusses the Non-Dimensionalization of the governing equations to simplify the analysis.

5. Flex Gloves - Bridging Communication Gaps with Technology

This project was done in the course of World of Engineering. We developed innovative Flex Gloves designed to bridge the communication gap for individuals with speech impairments. These gloves are equipped with flex sensors and motion detectors that capture the movements of sign language gestures. The data is then processed through a microcontroller, which translates these gestures into audible speech via a connected speaker.

Through Flex Gloves, we aim to provide a voice to those who communicate through sign language, fostering greater understanding and interaction in diverse social settings.
